abstract: Foot drop is a neuromuscular disorder that causes abnormal gait patterns.
  This study developed a pneumatically powered ankle-foot orthosis (AFO) to improve
  the gait patterns of patients with foot drop. We hypothesized that providing unilateral
  ankle dorsiflexion assistance during the swing phase would improve the kinematics
  and spatiotemporal gait parameters of such patients. Accordingly, this study aims
  to examine the efficacy of the proposed assistance system using a strategy for joint
  kinematics and spatiotemporal gait parameters (stride length, swing velocity, and
  stance phase ratio). The analysis results are expected to provide knowledge for
  better design and control of AFOs in patients with foot drop.
